Periodic Table

The periodic table is a tabular array of the chemical elements organized by atomic number, from the element with the lowest atomic number, hydrogen, to the element with the highest atomic number, oganesson. The atomic number of an element is the number of protons in the nucleus of an atom of that element.

Balancing Equation

The study of chemical reactions is a crucial part of middle school chemistry education, as it introduces the concept of reactants and products. Students learn that the same atoms present in the reactants are also present in the products, and they discover how different combinations of reactants can produce a variety of products. ASP provides an effective method to model chemical reactions and determine if they are balanced. The reactants and products are first labeled and added as knowledge, followed by the addition of information about the number of molecules for each substance. The number of atoms in each substance is also added to this knowledge, which is then used to check if the reaction is balanced. This approach allows students to gain a deeper understanding of chemical reactions and provides an interactive way to explore different chemical reactions and their outcomes. .

Density of Elements

This topic talks about density and explains why some solids sink in a liquid and other float. It also talks about why some liquid sink in another liquid and others do not. This topic gives interesting examples to demonstrate the students how difference in density causes object to float and sink. Density is calculated by dividing mass of a substance with its volume. Mass and volume of a substance can be entered as knowledge and we can write a rule to calculate its density. Then density of different substance can be checked to see which one is more dense. An object floats on another if its density is less or sinks otherwise.
